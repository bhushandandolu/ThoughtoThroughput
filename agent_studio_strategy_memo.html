§ 01

Why I'm writing this to myself

I have a strong PM income, two existing capital plays (Bengaluru land, rental setup), an active F&O book, a wife and a daughter in Class 3. I do not need this venture to work in order to eat. That's a feature, not a bug — it lets me be ruthless about kill criteria.

This memo exists so that 90 days from now, when I am either excited about a working prototype or frustrated by API bills, I can come back and check what I actually believed at the start. Future me is not allowed to rewrite past me's assumptions silently.

§ 02

What I got wrong on the first pass

I framed this as: "AI agent orchestration that automates the entire media production workflow — story writers, character artists, scene builders, screenplay, editors, directors, critiques." Six things wrong with that:

  1. That's a Bollywood-studio problem, not a creator problem. Solo creators don't think in screenplays. Studios do — and they have ~10 vendors already.
  2. The "specialist agents in every function" architecture is already shipping. DeeVid, Invideo Agent, RoboNeo, HeyGen Video Agent, LTX Studio, Showrunner. I would be late to a category, not early.
  3. Foundation video models (Sora, Veo 4, Runway Gen-4, Kling) are commoditising the hardest part of the stack. If my product depends on being the model, I lose. If it depends on the workflow around the model, I have a chance.
  4. "End-to-end automated movie" is a $5–20M Series A play. I would burn ₹2–4 crore before a single paying customer. Not bootstrappable.
  5. None of my actual strengths apply. I'm a Senior PM in e-commerce/SFCC with program management and content/playbook chops. I am not an ML researcher. The version of this idea I first described requires someone I am not.
  6. My time budget is 10–15 hrs/week. I cannot out-build a funded 8-person team. I have to out-niche them.
Kill the broad version "End-to-end AI movie studio" is the wrong shape for me. It's the right shape for someone with $5M and an ML team in Bangalore. That's not me. Stop pretending it is.

Lead ICP, fully specified

Hyderabad/Bengaluru small-and-mid creative shops and digital ad agencies (5–25 people) who churn out 30–200 short ad creatives per month for D2C brands and local advertisers. I can find ~50 of these inside my own LinkedIn second-degree network in Hyderabad alone. Sales motion: founder-led, by introduction, lunch meetings on weekends. Not Google Ads.

§ 05

The wedge product — 90-day MVP

Working name: Studio Tantra (placeholder; bikeshed later).

One-line pitch: "Upload your brand mascot or hero shot. We generate consistent ad variants across 6 Indian languages, 3 aspect ratios, and 4 hooks — billed per finished asset."

Inputs the customer provides

  • Reference image of mascot/hero/product
  • Brand voice doc or 5 prior ads
  • Campaign brief (1 paragraph)
  • Target language list

Outputs the platform delivers

  • 6–18 ad variants (language × aspect × hook permutations)
  • Each one character-consistent with the reference
  • Lip-synced regional dub where there's a presenter
  • QA-graded against brand rules + Meta/YouTube spec
  • Deliverable in 4–24 hours, not 2 weeks

What I deliberately exclude in v1

  • Long-form video (>90 seconds)
  • Live-action film / drama / narrative storytelling
  • Original music composition (use ElevenLabs / Suno API only)
  • Custom model training (use APIs end-to-end)
  • The "build my own creator channel" idea — that's Year 2, not now

§ 10

Commercial & operational challenges

Commercial

  • Variable COGS. Foundation-model price changes can wreck margin overnight. Mitigation: multi-vendor routing, hard per-job budget caps, weekly margin review.
  • Customer concentration risk. First 5 customers will be 80% of revenue. One leaves, I lose 20% MRR.
  • Long sales cycles even for SMB. Indian agencies negotiate. Expect 3–6 weeks from intro to first paid asset.
  • Credit hoarding / breakage. Customers buy big tier, use little. Looks like good revenue but is actually deferred liability. Need a credit-expiry policy.
  • Outcome attribution. If my asset performs badly in a Meta ad, the agency blames the tool, not the targeting. Need hooks for client to tag wins.
  • White-label pressure. Agencies will ask to put their logo on my output. Yes for Agency tier; no for Starter / Studio.

Operational

  • Quality variance. Same prompt, different runs, different outputs. Will frustrate B2B customers expecting deterministic delivery. The Critique/QA agent is my answer.
  • Support load. "Why does my mascot's nose change shape in scene 2?" — this is a real ticket I'll get on day one. Need a clear regenerate-credit policy.
  • Indian language QA. Telugu lip-sync is harder than English. I'll need 2–3 native QA reviewers per language at some volume. Solvable but not free.
  • Compliance. Meta's AI-content disclosure rules, India's DPDP Act for stored customer assets, IT Act 2000 intermediary obligations. Get a CA + a lawyer's 1-hour review before launch.
  • Founder concentration. If I'm hit by a bus, the business stops. Must document SOPs from day 1.
  • Time arbitrage with day job. vidaXL has IP / moonlighting policies. Read the contract this week. Likely fine because this is non-competing, but verify in writing.

§ 13

Roadmap — Y1, Y2, Y3

Year 1 — survive and prove

  • M1–M3: Build MVP. 6 agents. One ICP (Hyderabad ad shops). Sign 3 design partners at ₹0 in exchange for case studies.
  • M4–M6: Convert design partners to paid. Onboard 5 more agencies. Launch Studio + Agency tiers.
  • M7–M9: 15–25 paying customers. Hit ₹3–5L MRR. Hire 1 part-time engineer.
  • M10–M12: 30–50 paying customers. ₹6–10L MRR. Cash-positive month-on-month.

Year 2 — expand the wedge

  • Add D2C performance brands as second ICP.
  • Expand language coverage to 8 Indian + 4 SE Asian.
  • Build the "creator channel" idea — but only as a marketing flywheel, not a separate venture. Use it to publish demos in regional languages.
  • Consider raising a small angel round (₹2–4 cr) only if growth data supports it.

Year 3 — defend or exit

  • Defensibility audit. If the QA layer + workflow + India language tuning is real moat, raise seed.
  • If foundation models have caught up and commoditised the space, sell to a creative-tools company or wind down with dignity.
  • Either way, do not pretend year 3 is still year 1.
§ 14

Kill criteria — when I walk away

Pre-committing these because future-me will be motivated to ignore the data.

  1. Month 4: If I do not have at least 3 unpaid design partners using the product weekly, kill.
  2. Month 6: If I do not have at least 5 paying customers (≥ ₹4,999/mo each), kill.
  3. Month 9: If MRR < ₹2L or gross margin < 30%, pause and re-architect; if both fail, kill.
  4. Month 12: If MRR < ₹6L or I am routinely working >20 hrs/week on this, kill or sell.
  5. Anytime: If a major foundation-model vendor ships an India-localised creative agent that does 80% of what I do at < ₹0.5/asset, accept and exit gracefully.
  6. Anytime: If my day-job performance reviews drop a tier — kill. The salary is the foundation, not the side hustle.
Promise to myself I will treat these kill criteria as data, not as failure. The point of this venture is optionality, not validation of my ego.
