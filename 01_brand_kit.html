Brand Identity Kit

An orchestra
of agents.

A B2B AI media production platform built for Indian SMB ad shops, regional content studios, and D2C marketers. Where six specialist agents work in concert — and the founder doesn't.

§ 01 · Brand idea

The orchestra, not the soloist.

Most "AI video" tools sell a single magic button. This brand sells the opposite: a system of specialists, each accountable for one part of the work — brief parsing, identity locking, scene rendering, dub, edit, critique. The brand voice is editorial and confident, not techno-futuristic. The visual language borrows from independent magazines and design-studio identities, not from SaaS dashboards or web3 chrome.

Brand pillars

  • Specialist over generalist. We name and credit each agent. We do not pretend a single model does everything.
  • Production discipline. Every output passes through a critique gate. Quality is checked, not promised.
  • Indian craft, global polish. We are built for Telugu, Tamil, Hindi, English. The output ships globally.
  • Founder-led trust. No 24/7 chatbot. A human approves the brief and signs the invoice.
§ 02 · Wordmark

Wordmark system.

The wordmark is built in two registers. Studio sits on top in monospaced caps — small, precise, structural. The product name sits below in editorial serif, with one letter italicized in saffron for character and asymmetry. The italicized letter is the brand's signature device. It survives reduction. It works in motion.

§ 03 · Monogram

The mark.

For app icons, favicons, agent badges, and watermarks. The monogram is built on a single italicized "a" inside a structural frame — a literal echo of the wordmark. The frame represents the orchestrator; the italic "a" represents the agent doing the work inside it.

§ 04 · Agent system

Six agents, six marks.

Each specialist agent gets its own glyph. The glyphs share a common geometric language — a 24×24 grid, single-weight strokes, one fill color, one accent. They sit inside the orchestrator frame. This iconography is what survives a name change unchanged — it's the most portable part of the system.

Note: the Critique agent's frame is intentionally drawn in the saffron-deep color, signaling its role as the quality-gate authority. This is the only agent that can reject output and trigger a regeneration. Treating it as visually privileged in the brand reinforces the product positioning: every asset is checked, not promised.

§ 05 · Color

A warm-paper palette.

Anchored in saffron — Indian heritage, but at a hue (#d97706) that reads as confident editorial accent globally, not as a flag-waving cliché. The neutrals are bone-paper, not pure white, to give the brand a tactile, printed-publication feel. Ink is true black for legibility. The signal colors stay muted to keep the system from feeling like a status dashboard.

Usage rules

  • Saffron earns its place — used for accents, italic letters, the Critique-agent frame, and primary CTAs only. Never as a body color, never on more than 10% of any surface.
  • Carbon is the default text color. Bone is the default surface. This is a reading brand first, an interface brand second.
  • Avoid gradients. Avoid drop shadows. Avoid glassmorphism. The brand is print-confident, not SaaS-glossy.

§ 07 · Voice & tone

Editorial confidence, no hype.

We sound likeWe don't sound like
"Six agents. One critique gate. Your campaign ships in 18 hours." "Unleash the power of AI to revolutionize your creative workflow!"
"We do not generate full films. We generate ad campaigns." "The future of filmmaking is here."
"Telugu, Tamil, Hindi, Marathi, Kannada, Bangla, English. In that order of release." "Reach a global audience in any language!"
"If the Critique agent rejects an asset, we regenerate at no charge." "Premium quality, every time, guaranteed!"

Three rules for any copy

  1. Specific over evocative. Numbers, agent names, language lists. Never "transformative."
  2. What we don't do is part of what we do. Saying "we don't generate full films" is positioning, not weakness.
  3. The customer is competent. No condescension. No emoji. No exclamation marks.

The point: if you do switch names, ~70% of this system carries forward. The italicized accent-letter device, the saffron palette, the agent glyph system, the typography pairing, the voice rules — all of these are conceptually independent of the word "Tantra." That's deliberate. It's also the most generous thing I can offer given my disagreement with the name.
